Zhijie Wang

王志杰
Ph.D. Student in Environmental Engineering @ UW–Madison

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, with a minor in Computer Science. I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Mohan Qin, and my current work combines membrane transport modeling with scientific machine learning, with particular interest in physics-informed neural networks and Fourier neural networks for solving transport PDEs.

Before joining UW–Madison, I earned my M.S. in Environmental Engineering from Tongji University, where I was advised by Dr. Fan Lyu and worked on membrane and electrodialysis systems for resource recovery from waste streams. I also completed an Erasmus+ exchange semester at the University of Florence and an internship at NOVAMONT S.p.A. in Italy. My undergraduate education in Environmental Engineering at Shandong University built the foundation for my later work in environmental systems, materials, and biochemical processes.

Research Interests

I work at the intersection of environmental engineering, transport modeling, and scientific machine learning. I am especially interested in developing physically grounded computational tools for membrane systems, from mechanistic PDE formulations to physics-informed neural networks and Fourier neural networks / neural operators that can accelerate prediction and improve scientific understanding.

Scientific Modeling for Membrane Transport Systems

Physical System

Pressure-driven membranes, transmembrane mass transfer, and contaminant transport.

Mathematical Model

PDE-based descriptions of coupled transport, partitioning, and membrane boundary effects.

Learning Framework

PINNs, Fourier neural networks, and operator-learning based surrogate models.

Applications

Water treatment, membrane process design, and data-driven engineering analysis.

My Previous Research

My previous research has centered on membrane-based separations, resource recovery, and environmental process engineering. At UW–Madison, I study nutrient transport across intrinsically charged membranes and develop theoretical and numerical frameworks for amphoteric ion transport, while also exploring alternative PPIN-style approaches for PDE solving. In parallel, I investigate the fate transformation of low-micrometer microplastics during pressure-driven membrane filtration and build AI-assisted workflows that integrate image segmentation with Raman spectrum classification.

During my master’s research at Tongji University, I designed anaerobic membrane bioreactor and electrodialysis systems for producing and recovering organic acids from municipal organic waste, and studied the transport of salts, nutrients, and organics together with membrane fouling behavior. I also worked on the degradation of bio-based plastics during composting and anaerobic digestion. Earlier, as an undergraduate researcher, I studied biochar-mediated enhancement of anaerobic methanogenesis and PAH degradation.

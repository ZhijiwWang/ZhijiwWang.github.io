About

I focus on mass transfer modeling in membrane filtration systems and am currently exploring physics-informed neural networks and Fourier neural networks for solving transport PDEs.

Chemistry, modeling, and machine learning — together.

My background combines environmental chemistry, membrane transport, and computational methods. I am interested in building reliable, physics-guided models for complex engineering systems, with applications in water treatment, transport prediction, and scientific computing.

Research Focus

A concise overview of the areas I am currently working on and developing further.

Developing models to understand mass transfer behavior in membrane filtration systems, with emphasis on transport mechanisms and PDE-based descriptions.

Scientific Machine Learning

Applying machine learning methods to physical systems, especially where domain knowledge can improve robustness, interpretability, and predictive performance.

PINNs & Fourier Networks

Exploring physics-informed neural networks and Fourier-based neural architectures for solving transport equations and accelerating scientific modeling workflows.

Environmental Applications

Connecting computational tools with environmental engineering problems, especially in water treatment and complex transport processes.
